Niagara Elopement Photography

What is an Elopement?

Want to get married without all the stress, big guest list and huge price tag of a traditional wedding? Then eloping in Niagara might be perfect for you.

Over the years I've photographed elopements with anywhere from just the couple and two witnesses, to intimate celebrations of 20 guests. Sometimes it's just the two of you and I even get to be one of the witnesses who signs the marriage license. Elopements are a great way to celebrate with your closest family and friends while keeping things relaxed, personal and easy to plan.

Why elope in Niagara?

The Niagara region has always been a popular spot for weddings, and Niagara Falls is known as the honeymoon capital of the world. In the last few years, elopements here have exploded in popularity with couples who want to get married in a beautiful location but keep things small and intimate.

Niagara Falls and Niagara-on-the-Lake offer an incredible variety of places to say your vows. Some couples choose to get eloped right in front of the Falls and use the waterfall as their backdrop. Others prefer one of the nearby parks like Oakes Garden Theatre or the Botanical Gardens, and some even have their ceremony on the Hornblower boat while the Falls roar just a few hundred feet away. Historic Niagara-on-the-Lake adds vintage hotels, charming parks and some of the most beautiful wineries and vineyards in Canada to the mix.


Planning an Elopement in Niagara?

Often I'm the first vendor couples contact when they start planning their Niagara elopement. I've photographed well over 100 elopements around the region and I'm always happy to share tips I've learned over the years.

I can help you with recommendations on ceremony locations, the best spots for photos afterward, and trusted vendors for ministers or officiants, flowers, makeup and hair. I can also suggest restaurants and venues that work well for a small reception or dinner after your ceremony.

Niagara Elopement FAQ

How many guests can we have at our elopement?

Most of the elopements I photograph in Niagara range from just the couple and two witnesses up to around 20 guests. If your guest count is a bit higher but you still want things to feel intimate, we can chat about what will work best for your locations and timeline.

Do you help with locations and planning?

Yes. I've photographed well over 100 elopements around the Niagara region and I'm always happy to help with location suggestions, timing, and vendor recommendations for officiants, flowers, hair and makeup and restaurants for a small celebration afterward.

What happens if the weather is bad on our elopement day?

Niagara weather can be unpredictable, but we'll always have a backup plan. That might mean using covered spots, umbrellas, shifting your portrait time or choosing an indoor option nearby. Some of my favourite elopement photos have been taken in mist, fog or light rain.

Do you photograph couples travelling in from out of town?

Absolutely. Many of my elopement couples travel to Niagara Falls from other parts of Canada or the U.S. I can help you plan locations and timing remotely so you arrive with a clear, relaxed plan for your day.
